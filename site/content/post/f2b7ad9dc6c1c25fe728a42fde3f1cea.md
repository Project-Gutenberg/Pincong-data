> Nazi Germany   
> In Nazi Germany, the term was revived to justify the punishment of kin (relatives, spouse) for the offence of a family member. In this form of _Sippenhaft_ the relatives of persons accused of crimes against the state were held to share the responsibility for those crimes and subject to arrest and sometimes execution. Many people who had committed no crimes were arrested and punished under _Sippenhaft_ decrees introduced after the failed [20 July plot]( "https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/20_July_plot") to assassinate [Adolf Hitler]( "https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Adolf_Hitler") in July 1944.  
>   
> Examples of _Sippenhaft_ being used as a threat exist within the _[Wehrmacht]( "https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Wehrmacht")_ from around 1943. Soldiers accused of having "blood impurities" or soldiers conscripted from outside of Germany also began to have their families threatened and punished with _Sippenhaft_. An example is the case of _[Panzergrenadier]( "https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Panzergrenadier")_ Wenzeslaus Leiss, who was accused of desertion on the [Eastern Front]( "https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Eastern_Front_(World_War_II)") in December 1942. After the [Düsseldorf]( "https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/D%C3%BCsseldorf") [Gestapo]( "https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Gestapo") discovered supposed Polish links in the Leiss family, in February 1943 his wife, two-year-old daughter, two brothers, sister and brother-in-law were arrested and executed at [Sachsenhausen concentration camp]( "https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Sachsenhausen_concentration_camp"). By 1944, several general and individual directives were ordered within divisions and corps, threatening troops with consequences against their families. After 20 July 1944 these threats were extended to include all German troops, in particular, German commanders. A decree of February 1945 threatened death to the relatives of military commanders who showed what Hitler regarded as cowardice or defeatism in the face of the enemy. After [the surrender of Königsberg]( "https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Battle_of_K%C3%B6nigsberg") to the Soviets in April 1945, the family of the German commander General [Otto Lasch]( "https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Otto_Lasch") were arrested. These arrests were publicized in the _[Völkischer Beobachter]( "https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/V%C3%B6lkischer_Beobachter")_.  
>   
> After the failure of the [20 July plot]( "https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/20_July_plot"), the [SS]( "https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Schutzstaffel") chief [Heinrich Himmler]( "https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Heinrich_Himmler") told a meeting of [_Gauleiter_s]( "https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Gauleiter") in [Posen]( "https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Pozna%C5%84") that he would "introduce absolute responsibility of kin ... a very old custom practiced among our forefathers". According to Himmler, this practice had existed among the ancient [Teutons]( "https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Teutons"). "When they placed a family under the ban and declared it outlawed or when there was a [blood feud]( "https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Blood_feud") in the family, they were utterly consistent. ... This man has committed treason; his blood is bad; there is traitor's blood in him; that must be wiped out. And in the blood feud the entire clan was wiped out down to the last member. And so, too, will [Count Stauffenberg]( "https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Claus_von_Stauffenberg")'s family be wiped out down to the last member."  
>   
> Accordingly, the members of the family of von Stauffenberg (the officer who had planted the bomb that failed to kill Hitler) were all under suspicion. His wife, [Nina Schenk Gräfin von Stauffenberg]( "https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Nina_Schenk_Gr%C3%A4fin_von_Stauffenberg"), was sent to [Ravensbrück concentration camp]( "https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Ravensbr%C3%BCck_concentration_camp") (she survived and lived until 2006). His brother Alexander, who knew nothing of the plot and was serving with the [_Wehrmacht_ in Greece]( "https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/German_occupation_of_Greece"), was also sent to a concentration camp. Similar punishments were meted out to the relatives of [Carl Goerdeler]( "https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Carl_Goerdeler"), [Henning von Tresckow]( "https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Henning_von_Tresckow"), [Adam von Trott zu Solz]( "https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Adam_von_Trott_zu_Solz") and many other conspirators. [Erwin Rommel]( "https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Erwin_Rommel") opted to commit suicide rather than be tried for his suspected role in the plot in part because he knew that his wife and children would suffer well before his own all-but-certain conviction and execution.  
>   
> After the 20 July plot, numerous families connected to the Soviet-sponsored [League of German Officers]( "https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/National_Committee_for_a_Free_Germany#League_of_German_Officers") made up of German prisoners of war, such as those of [von Seydlitz]( "https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Walther_von_Seydlitz-Kurzbach") and [Paulus]( "https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Friedrich_Paulus"), were also arrested. Unlike a number of the 20 July conspirators families, those arrested for connection to the League were not released after a few months but remained in prison until the end of the war. Younger children of arrested plotters were not jailed but sent to orphanages under new names: von Stauffenberg's children were renamed "Meister".

> **族誅**是一種將受刑人[連坐]( "https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/%E9%80%A3%E5%9D%90")其族處以死刑，為古代社會的殘酷刑罰。而族就是有血緣關係的親屬之合稱[\[1\]]( "https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/%E6%97%8F%E8%AA%85#cite_note-1")。此刑法亦針對以家庭為本位傳統社會，例如古時的中國[\[2\]]( "https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/%E6%97%8F%E8%AA%85#cite_note-2")。當其中一位家庭成員犯下通敵[叛國]( "https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/%E5%8F%9B%E5%9C%8B")、欺君犯上、密謀造反等滔天[死罪]( "https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/%E6%AD%BB%E5%88%91")時，當權者為免除後患、斬草除根就會對犯事者施行此嚴刑以鞏固自身政權。  
>   
> 與中國同屬[儒家文化圈]( "https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/%E5%84%92%E5%AE%B6%E6%96%87%E5%8C%96%E5%9C%88")的[朝鮮]( "https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/%E6%9C%9D%E9%AE%AE")、[日本]( "https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/%E6%97%A5%E6%9C%AC")和[越南]( "https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/%E8%B6%8A%E5%8D%97")，亦曾使用這一種刑罰。而朝鮮制度中對「族」的定義與中國制度中的定義是幾近一樣[\[3\]]( "https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/%E6%97%8F%E8%AA%85#cite_note-3")。  
>   
> 在中國歷史中，族誅始於[商朝]( "https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/%E5%95%86%E6%9C%9D")。當時被稱為[劓殄]( "https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/%E5%8A%93%E6%AE%84")，乃族誅的初形，斬殺犯罪者及其後代之刑。[秦朝]( "https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/%E7%A7%A6%E6%9C%9D")時發展為誅三族、五族、七族。[隋朝]( "https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/%E9%9A%8B%E6%9C%9D")時被[隋文帝]( "https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/%E9%9A%8B%E6%96%87%E5%B8%9D")廢除，但在[隋煬帝]( "https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/%E9%9A%8B%E7%85%AC%E5%B8%9D")復行並擴至誅九族[\[4\]]( "https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/%E6%97%8F%E8%AA%85#cite_note-4")[\[5\]]( "https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/%E6%97%8F%E8%AA%85#cite_note-5")。
