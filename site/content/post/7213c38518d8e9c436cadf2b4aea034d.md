The [Hong Kong]( "https://www.theguardian.com/world/hong-kong") government has agreed to pay millions of pounds to a discreet London-based PR firm in a bid to counter coverage of the territory in the international media.  
  
Consulum, which has also represented Saudi leader [Mohammed bin Salman]( "https://www.theguardian.com/world/2018/oct/19/saudi-arabia-pays-uk-firms-millions-to-boost-image"), was [awarded the £5m one-year contract]( "https://www.info.gov.hk/gia/general/202006/29/P2020062900376.htm") to improve Hong Kong’s reputation on Monday – the same day that China passed a [new security law]( "https://www.theguardian.com/world/2020/jun/30/china-passes-controversial-hong-kong-national-security-law") targeting local pro-democracy activists.  
  
The Mayfair-based PR business was founded by Tim Ryan and Matthew Gunther Bushell, two former employees of Bell Pottinger, an agency that has been criticised for representing some governments and leaders that other businesses would not touch.  
  
The administration said it needed the support of a PR agency because of reputational damage caused by recent protests. It accepted it had failed to “mobilise the community to support law enforcement actions and condemn intimidation, doxxing, vandalism and the criminal and violent behaviour of rioters” during last year’s protests, in which protesters and police clashed regularly.

香港政府已同意向一家谨慎的伦敦公关公司支付数百万英镑，以对抗国际媒体对该地区的报道。  
  
Consulum，一间同时为沙特阿拉伯领袖穆罕默德·本·萨勒曼（Mohammed bin Salman）代理的公关公司，在周一获得了500万英镑的一年合同，以提高香港的声誉。同一天，中国通过了一项针对当地民主运动人士的国安法。  
  
总部位于梅费尔的公关业务，由贝尔·珀丁格的两位前雇员蒂姆·瑞安和马修·冈瑟·布谢尔创立。该公司曾因代表某些政府和领导人不愿与其他公司接触而受到批评。  
  
政府表示，由于最近的抗议活动损害了声誉，因此需要公关公司的支持。它接受它在去年的抗议活动中未能“动员社区支持执法行动，并谴责恐吓，捣蛋，故意破坏以及暴乱者的犯罪和暴力行为”，抗议者和警察经常发生冲突。
