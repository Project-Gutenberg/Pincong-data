**Factors Affecting Bail Amounts**  
**There is no fixed price for bail in Australia.** The amount varies considerably based on several important factors that courts carefully consider when determining bail amounts. These include:  
**The severity of the alleged offence** – Generally, the more serious the offence, the higher the bail amount. Indictable offences such as violent crimes, drug trafficking or fraud may attract significantly higher bail amounts than summary offences.  
**The accused person’s financial circumstances** – Courts consider what is reasonable and proportionate to the individual’s financial situation. An amount that would be insignificant to a wealthy person might be impossible for someone with limited means to pay.  
**The perceived risk of non-compliance** – If there are concerns that the accused may not appear in court when required, the court may set a higher bail amount as an incentive for compliance.  
**Criminal history** – A person’s previous interactions with the justice system, particularly whether they have previously committed bail breaches, will influence the bail costs.  
While specific amounts vary widely based on individual circumstances, there is no set range for bail amounts in Australia. For serious offences, it has been the experience of legal professionals that figures around **$20,000** have been sufficient to secure bail. The amount could be lower or significantly higher depending on the specific factors of the case and the court’s assessment of risk.  
The [Bail Act 2013](https://zhida.zhihu.com/search?content_id=741381965&content_type=Answer&match_order=1&q=Bail+Act+2013&zhida_source=entity) provides the legislative framework that guides courts in setting appropriate bail conditions, including financial requirements. Under these provisions, bail conditions must be reasonable, proportionate to the offence, appropriate to address any bail concerns, and no more onerous than necessary.
